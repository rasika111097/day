Here is the plant One

Doll's Eyes

Actaea pachypoda

Published on: 2023-05-15

Actaea pachypoda, commonly called white baneberry or “doll’s eyes,” is a shade‑loving perennial native to eastern North America. All parts of the plant are poisonous—especially the bright white berries and the roots—causing symptoms like burning mouth, stomach cramps, dizziness, and even cardiac effects if ingested in larger amounts. The berries are harmless to birds, which eat them and spread the seeds, while short‑tongued bees collect pollen from the spring flower clusters. It’s a great ornamental for woodland gardens, but keep it away from children and pets because of its high toxicity . The plant in the image is most commonly known as white baneberry or doll's-eyes (Actaea pachypoda). It is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America, primarily found in rich, moist woodlands.

Rafflesia aro

giant padma

Published on: 2023-05-15

The plant in the image is the Rafflesia arnoldii, which is commonly known in English as the corpse flower or giant padma. This species is notable for producing the largest individual flower on Earth. It is a parasitic flowering plant found in the rainforests of Sumatra and Borneo. The flower has a strong and unpleasant odor of decaying flesh, which attracts pollinators like carrion beetles and flies..
